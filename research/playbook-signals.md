# Playbook Signals — What This Research Means for 100Hires

> This document connects the research findings directly to 100Hires.
> It is not a finished playbook. It is the foundation one would be built on.

---

## About 100Hires

100Hires is an AI-powered Applicant Tracking System rated #1 for startups and small businesses by Forbes. The product sits in a competitive B2B SaaS category where buyers search terms like "best ATS for startups" and "AI hiring software." This is exactly the category where AI search is already reshaping how buyers discover and evaluate solutions.

---

## Signal 1: The ATS Category Is a High-Trust, High-Consideration Purchase

Kevin Indig's research found that users turn to AI search for research-phase queries, then verify with Reddit, YouTube, and organic results before deciding. Hiring software is exactly this type of purchase — nobody buys an ATS impulsively.

**Action:** 100Hires needs to be present in both the AI discovery phase (AI Overviews, ChatGPT responses) and the verification phase (Reddit threads, YouTube comparisons, G2 reviews). Content strategy should map to both.

---

## Signal 2: Reddit Is More Valuable Than Most B2B SaaS Companies Realize

Ross Simmonds' data shows Reddit is now cited more frequently in AI Overviews than G2 and Capterra for bottom-of-funnel B2B queries. For ATS software, buyers are already on Reddit asking "what ATS do you use" and "is 100Hires worth it."

**Action:** A Reddit presence in hiring and HR subreddits — genuinely helpful, not promotional — would create AI citation opportunities that a G2 listing cannot. This is currently underexploited by most ATS companies.

---

## Signal 3: Specific Problem Content Will Outperform Generic Category Content

Matt Diggity and Mike King both show that AI rewards content that directly answers specific questions at a passage level. Generic content like "what is an applicant tracking system" is increasingly answered by AI without a click. Specific content like "how to track candidates across multiple job openings without losing context" is more likely to be cited and clicked.

**Action:** 100Hires content should target the specific pain points of startup founders and small business HR teams — the moments just before they decide they need an ATS — not category-level keywords.

---

## Signal 4: Brand Consistency Across Platforms Is Now a Technical Requirement

Victoria Kurichenko's research shows LLMs learn about brands from their consistency across Google Business Profile, LinkedIn, directories, and review platforms. Ensuring that 100Hires' product description, value proposition, and category positioning are consistent across all platforms is directly tied to how accurately AI systems represent the brand.

**Action:** An audit of how 100Hires is described across G2, Capterra, Product Hunt, LinkedIn, and Crunchbase — and making that language consistent — is an immediate, low-effort AI SEO win.

---

## Signal 5: Original Data Creates Uncopyable Content

Gael Breton's most powerful workflow: use AI to identify information that exists in video/audio but has never been indexed as text. For 100Hires, this could mean transcribing founder interviews, anonymized customer conversations, or hiring outcome data — content that contains genuine insight no competitor has.

**Action:** A series of "what we learned from X hiring conversations" posts, built from real internal data, would be genuinely uncopyable and highly citable by AI systems.

---

## Three Content Priorities This Research Supports

1. **Structured problem-specific content** targeting the exact questions startup founders ask before buying an ATS — written for passage-level AI extraction, not just human reading.

2. **Reddit presence** in hiring and HR communities — genuinely helpful, not promotional — to create AI citation signals where LLMs look most.

3. **Brand consistency audit** across all third-party platforms to ensure AI systems describe 100Hires accurately when buyers ask about it.
