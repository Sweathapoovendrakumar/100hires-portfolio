## Key Insight

Internal communication is the most underrated marketing lever right now. Teams that communicate cross-functionally are outperforming teams with better tactics but worse coordination.

---

## Post Content

I've been talking a lot about how to move marketing teams in a different direction as we come to grips with how changes in marketing strategy impact how we work as teams.

Changing strategy is hard. Rethinking team operations and team structure for a new ecosystem? That's a nightmare.

There's not going to be any quick answer to any of this. And in my opinion, it will take a good 5 years for this to sort out.

But the one thing you should do is communicate... a lot.

It is NO different than any other complex relationship that is dealing with a significant problem or challenge. The process of communication is everything.

If we want to "break the silos" of how marketing teams work, we can't live in a silo of communication. It's a proportional relationship.

Whatever strategy or tactics you have, it's nowhere near as important as the ever-boring internal comms are right now.

---

## Annotation

This has a specific implication for content strategy execution: a company can have perfect knowledge of AI SEO best practices and still fail to execute them if the SEO, content, and brand teams are operating toward different KPIs. The organizational bottleneck is more common than the knowledge bottleneck.
