# Content Collection Methodology

## YouTube Transcripts

**Tool:** Tactiq.io YouTube Transcript API (free tier)
**Method:** Each YouTube URL was submitted to Tactiq.io which uses the YouTube Data API to retrieve captions and return them as structured text with timestamps.
**Processing:** Raw transcripts were reviewed manually, key insights extracted, and each file annotated with ideas relevant to AI-powered SEO and B2B SaaS application.

**Videos processed:**
- Matt Diggity — ~2,300 words
- Ross Simmonds — ~4,100 words
- Kevin Indig — ~6,200 words
- Jack Chambers-Ward — ~9,800 words
- Gael Breton — ~10,400 words
- Mike King — ~5,600 words

## LinkedIn Posts

**Method:** Manual collection
**Reason:** LinkedIn's API does not permit programmatic access to post content for personal accounts under standard terms of service. Manual collection is the standard approach used in professional research and competitive intelligence workflows.

## Expert Selection Process

**Step 1 — Initial list:** Generated 20+ candidate names across AI SEO, content production, and B2B SaaS marketing.

**Step 2 — Signal quality filter:** Each candidate evaluated against:
- Does this person produce original research or document real results?
- Are they among the first Google results for "AI SEO expert"? If yes, deprioritized.
- Does their content directly address B2B SaaS use cases?

**Step 3 — Platform balance:** Final 10 ensures mix of YouTube creators, podcast hosts, and LinkedIn practitioners.

## What Was Deliberately Excluded

- **Neil Patel** — Too high-profile; likely in every other submission. Low specificity.
- **Ahrefs/Semrush official channels** — Vendor content optimized for product promotion, not practitioner insight.
- **Generic AI SEO roundups** — Aggregation is not research. Only primary voices included.
